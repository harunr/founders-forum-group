Our Story

Founders Forum started in 2005 with a simple thought in mind – to create the perfect environment for collaboration, establishing a credible, peer-to-peer network of European entrepreneurs echoing the innovation unfolding in Silicon Valley.

Our community first convened at a 50-person event in London, where founders met with investors, CEOs and government leaders to discuss the future of industries, new technologies and business models.

Since then, we’ve hosted over 40 forums in more than 20 cities worldwide, from Los Angeles, Rio de Janeiro and Tel Aviv, to Mumbai, Paris and Hong Kong.

As we’ve expanded our global footprint, we’ve also built a group of businesses designed to support entrepreneurs at every stage – from pre-seed to post-exit.

Our Leaders

Brent Hoberman

Co-Founder & Executive Chair

Brent is Co-Founder and Executive Chair of Founders Forum Group, Founders Factory and firstminute capital. Previously, Brent co-founded Made.com in 2010, which went public last year with a valuation of $1.1bn, and lastminute.com in 1998, where he was CEO from its inception and sold it in 2005 to Sabre for $1.1bn. Brent has backed 9 unicorns at Seed stage and technology businesses Brent has co-founded have raised over $1bn, including Karakuri and Founders Intelligence – formerly FF Group’s entrepreneur-powered consultancy, recently acquired by Accenture.

Brent sits on the advisory boards of GoogleCloud, The Royal Academy, the UK Government Digital Service and the WEF Digital Europe Group. He was also a founding Champion of Change for The Global Tech Group, has been an advisor to four UK prime ministers, and in 2015, was awarded a CBE for his services to entrepreneurship.

Carolyn Dawson

CEO

Carolyn Dawson is Chief Executive of Founders Forum Group, overseeing all FF events and businesses, and steering the strategic development of our global network. Prior to Founders Forum Group, Carolyn was President at Informa Tech, a FTSE 100 UK company championing specialists in their core markets. Carolyn led a global portfolio of Digital Media, Research, Events and Training across a variety of technology sectors and presided over the joint venture between Founders Forum and Informa Tech, creating new media and event opportunities for the premier community of global entrepreneurs, CEOs and investors, prior to her assuming her role as FFG CEO. She has led London Tech Week for seven years, building its global profile and driving its mission to showcase the very best of technology across the UK. Carolyn is passionate about growing the tech community and ensuring it is open to all, to truly realise the positive impact of technology on our world and empower the next generation of entrepreneurs.
